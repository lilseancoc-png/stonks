        How is the grade computed?

        Each contract picks up a Spread, Delta, and Theta grade, then the overall verdict aggregates them:

        • 2+ bad → Poor contract
        • 1 bad → Mixed — proceed with caution
        • 2+ good → Good contract
        • otherwise → Acceptable

        A clear news tailwind or headwind can nudge an Acceptable verdict to Good or Poor based on the AI-summarized headline sentiment.

        Per-metric thresholds

        • Spread: Tight (≤5% of mid), Moderate (5–15%), Wide (>15%)
        • Delta: Balanced (0.40–0.70), Slightly OTM (0.30–0.40), Deep ITM (>0.70), Far OTM (<0.30)
        • Theta: Slow decay (<1% of mid/day), Normal (1–3%), Bleeding (>3%)
        • Liquidity (open interest): Thin (<10), Light (<100), Liquid (≥100)
        • 30d realized vol: Calm (bottom 30% of this name’s own history), Normal, Elevated (top 30%)

        YES / NO buy badge

        The binary badge is independent of the Good/Mixed/Poor verdict. It falls to NO immediately for any of these mechanical disqualifiers:

        • Wide spread, Far-OTM delta, or Bleeding theta
        • ≤3 days to expiry (gamma and theta are extreme)
        • Premium that is >80% time-value with <14 days to expiry

        Otherwise it scores news (±2), RSI + MACD + volume conviction (±1 each), and fundamentals verdict (±1). The score is multiplied by the option direction (+1 for calls, −1 for puts). Inside 30 DTE the score takes a −1 penalty — not a hard fail, but theta accelerates fast in the last month. It clears to YES when either: aligned score ≥+2 with no opposing signals, or two “good” mechanical grades with nothing opposing the direction.

        Volume conviction

        Today’s daily volume vs the trailing 20-day average, paired with today’s 1-day price move, sorts the print into one of four buckets:

        • Strong: large move (≥1.5%) on heavy volume (≥1.5× avg) — real conviction
        • Indecision: small move (<0.5%) on heavy volume — accumulation or distribution
        • Weak: large move on light volume (<0.7× avg) — treat with skepticism (a 10% after-hours pop on 1,000 shares is not 10% of conviction)
        • None: small move on light volume — nothing to react to

        Open positions: free-ride / roll rule

        On the portfolio side: if a position is ITM or up ≥100% with ≤40 DTE and conviction is still bullish, the recommendation flips to trim-to-cost (sell enough contracts to recover original cost, let the rest free-ride) or roll (extend the expiration / move the strike) instead of plain hold.

        All thresholds are simple heuristics, not optimal strategies. For information only — not investment advice.
